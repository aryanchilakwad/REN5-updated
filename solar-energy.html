Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about solar energy and our services

The cost of a solar installation varies based on several factors, including system size, equipment quality, and installation complexity. For residential systems, prices typically range from $15,000 to $30,000 before incentives. Commercial installations are priced based on specific business needs and energy requirements. Federal tax credits, local incentives, and financing options can significantly reduce your upfront costs. We provide detailed, transparent pricing in our personalized consultations.

From contract signing to system activation, the typical timeline is 2-3 months. The actual installation usually takes only 1-3 days, but permitting, utility approval, and inspection processes account for most of the timeline. Our project managers keep you informed at every stage and handle all paperwork and coordination with local authorities and utility companies to ensure a smooth process.

Solar systems are remarkably low-maintenance. Occasional cleaning (2-4 times per year) to remove dust and debris is typically sufficient to maintain optimal performance. In most climates, rainfall naturally cleans the panels. We offer maintenance plans that include professional cleaning, system inspections, and performance monitoring to ensure your system operates at peak efficiency throughout its lifespan.

Standard grid-tied solar systems will shut down during a utility power outage for safety reasons. However, systems with battery backup can continue to provide electricity during outages. We offer various energy storage solutions that can be tailored to your specific backup power needs, allowing you to maintain essential services or even full home functionality during grid disruptions.

In areas with net metering policies, excess electricity is fed back into the grid, and you receive credits on your utility bill. These credits can offset future periods when your system produces less than you consume. Some utilities offer full retail rate compensation, while others use alternative compensation methods. During your consultation, we'll explain the specific net metering policies in your area and how they affect your return on investment.
